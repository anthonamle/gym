General Terms

Here you will find explanations of common gym terms and concepts, their importance, and how to use them to build muscle.

RIR (Reps In Reserve)

RIR stands for Reps In Reserve, which refers to the number of repetitions you can still perform before reaching failure. It helps in gauging the intensity of your workout.

Importance: RIR is crucial for managing workout intensity and avoiding overtraining. It allows you to push your limits safely and effectively.

How to Use: To use RIR, estimate how many more reps you could perform before failure. For example, if you stop at 8 reps but could have done 2 more, your RIR is 2.

RIR 1 and RIR 0: RIR 1 means you have one rep left in the tank before failure, while RIR 0 means you have reached failure. RIR 1 is often used for strength training, while RIR 0 is used for hypertrophy.

Failure/Close to Failure

Training to failure means performing an exercise until you cannot complete another repetition with good form. Close to failure means stopping just short of this point.

Importance: Training to failure maximizes muscle recruitment and growth. It ensures that you are pushing your muscles to their limits.

How to Use: Use failure training sparingly to avoid overtraining. Incorporate it into your routine for specific sets or exercises to maximize muscle growth.

Difference Between Failure and RIR: Failure means you cannot perform another rep, while RIR indicates how many reps you have left before failure. Both are used to gauge workout intensity and ensure effective training.

Mechanical Tension

Mechanical tension refers to the force generated by muscles during resistance training. It is one of the primary drivers of muscle growth.

Importance: Mechanical tension is essential for muscle hypertrophy and strength gains. It stimulates muscle fibers and promotes growth.

How to Use: To maximize mechanical tension, focus on lifting heavy weights with proper form. Ensure that you are progressively overloading your muscles over time.

Hypertrophy

Hypertrophy is the increase in muscle size due to resistance training. It is achieved by creating micro-tears in muscle fibers, which then repair and grow stronger.

Importance: Hypertrophy is important for building muscle mass and improving overall strength and aesthetics.

How to Reach Hypertrophy: To achieve hypertrophy, focus on progressive overload, proper nutrition, and adequate rest. Incorporate a variety of exercises, rep ranges, and training intensities into your routine.

Full Range of Motion vs. Partial Reps

Full range of motion (ROM) involves moving a joint through its entire range during an exercise, while partial reps involve moving through only a portion of the range.

Advantages of Full ROM: Maximizes muscle activation, improves flexibility, and ensures balanced muscle development.

Disadvantages of Full ROM: Can be more challenging and may require lighter weights to maintain proper form.

Advantages of Partial Reps: Allows for heavier weights, targets specific parts of the muscle, and can be used to overcome sticking points.

Disadvantages of Partial Reps: May lead to imbalanced muscle development and reduced flexibility if overused.

How to Use: Incorporate both full ROM and partial reps into your training. Use full ROM for overall muscle development and partial reps to target specific areas or overcome plateaus.
